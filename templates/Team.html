How It All Began

My Why

In 2018, shortly after my 30 birthday, I suffered what the doctorâ€™s determined to be a transient ischemic attack or TIA. TIAs are informally known as mini-strokes. This occurred one-month prior to the birth of my daughter and resulted in an extensive hospital stay in which doctors tried to determine what could have caused this episode. It was probably the scariest moment of my life.

I left the hospital understanding that I needed to make lifestyle changes to improve my health, but as I researched health issues in my community, I learned that my experience was far from rare. As I began understanding more about health inequities, a desire to change my community began to take shape.

Coupled with my background in public policy and administration, I started to think about ways

that I could change my environment systemically. With no tech background and very little personal time (I had two kids under the age of two at the time), I taught myself how to develop a web application, which later became the building block for HEAL.

My goal is to transform communities so that all of our children can live better and healthier lives.
